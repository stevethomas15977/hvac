# Bid Process Use Case (Proposal Stage)

## Orchestrated flow

1. Intake bid package
2. Qualify whether to pursue
3. Extract requirements
4. Compare tool-path vs manufacturer-path selection
5. Generate proposal draft
6. Human approves release

A mechanical estimator at a manufacturer rep firm gets an invitation to bid for a new medical office building. Instead of manually sorting dozens of PDFs, they drag the full bid package into the platform. The system immediately organizes drawings, specs, and notes, then creates a project workspace with due dates and basic metadata. Within minutes, the estimator has a clean intake record instead of a scattered folder of files and emails.

The qualification agent runs next. It flags that the project includes the firm’s target equipment category, identifies approved manufacturers, and scores the opportunity as high-fit based on scope and timeline. The estimator reviews that recommendation, sees supporting citations from the bid documents, and chooses to pursue. That go/no-go decision is logged with rationale, so everyone can see why time and effort are being invested.

The extraction and selection workflow then begins. The tool pulls key requirements from schedules and specifications, normalizes units, and generates a tool-path model recommendation from catalog data. The estimator enters the manufacturer software result, and the system displays both outputs side-by-side, highlighting differences in capacity, constraints, or accessories. Because the comparison is explicit, the team resolves discrepancies quickly and confidently approves the final model selection.

With selection approved, the proposal composer creates a draft in the company’s standard format, including scope table, model descriptions, and pricing structure. The estimator and manager perform a final human review, make minor wording edits, and approve release. What used to take most of a day now happens in a focused, auditable flow that improves speed without removing human control where it matters most.
