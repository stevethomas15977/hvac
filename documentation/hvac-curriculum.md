Commercial HVAC Sales Representative Learning Curriculum

Designed for Business Graduates & AI Foundation Model Pre‑Training

---

1. Industry Foundations

A new HVAC sales rep must first understand the ecosystem they’re entering.

Core Modules

• HVAC Industry Overview — market segments, commercial vs. industrial, major OEMs, distribution channels
• Building Types — office, healthcare, education, industrial, hospitality
• Regulations & Codes — ASHRAE, IECC, EPA, local code compliance
• Sustainability Drivers — decarbonization, electrification, IAQ, ESG


Learning Outcomes

• Understand how HVAC impacts energy, comfort, and operations
• Recognize the economic and regulatory pressures shaping customer decisions


---

2. Technical HVAC Literacy (Non‑Engineer Track)

Business graduates don’t need to be engineers — but they must be dangerously competent.

Core Modules

• HVAC System Types — RTUs, chillers, boilers, VRF, AHUs, DOAS
• Heat Transfer Basics — conduction, convection, latent/sensible loads
• Airflow & Ductwork — CFM, static pressure, filtration
• Controls & BAS — sensors, VAV logic, trending, alarms
• Energy Efficiency — SEER, EER, IEER, COP, part‑load performance


Learning Outcomes

• Speak confidently with engineers, contractors, and facility managers
• Read basic mechanical drawings and equipment schedules
• Understand how systems fail, how they’re sized, and why they cost what they cost


---

3. Sales Engineering Fundamentals

This is where HVAC sales becomes a technical consultative profession.

Core Modules

• Load Calculation Concepts — not performing them, but interpreting them
• Equipment Selection Logic — matching system type to building need
• Proposal Engineering — scopes, alternates, exclusions
• Value Engineering — cost vs. performance tradeoffs
• Lifecycle Costing — energy modeling, payback, NPV


Learning Outcomes

• Build credible proposals
• Understand how engineers evaluate solutions
• Position your product as the “lowest total cost,” not the lowest price


---

4. Commercial Sales Skills

HVAC sales is relationship‑driven, technical, and long‑cycle.

Core Modules

• Territory Management — contractors, engineers, end users
• Pipeline Development — plan/spec vs. design/build
• Consultative Selling — discovery, pain points, ROI framing
• Bid Strategy — pre‑bid influence, alternates, negotiation
• Account Management — long‑term retention, service upsell


Learning Outcomes

• Build a predictable revenue pipeline
• Influence specifications early
• Navigate complex buying committees


---

5. Financial & Business Acumen

Commercial HVAC deals are capital‑intensive and financially scrutinized.

Core Modules

• Project Budgeting — equipment, labor, controls, commissioning
• Contract Structures — lump sum, GMP, T&M, performance contracts
• ROI Modeling — energy savings, maintenance savings
• Risk Management — lead times, supply chain, warranty


Learning Outcomes

• Build compelling financial justifications
• Understand how contractors and owners evaluate cost risk
• Speak the language of CFOs and facility directors


---

6. Field Experience & Apprenticeship

No HVAC sales rep succeeds without boots‑on‑the‑ground exposure.

Required Rotations

• Ride‑alongs with Technicians — troubleshooting, diagnostics
• Contractor Jobsite Visits — installation sequencing, safety
• Factory Training — product deep dives
• Engineer Lunch‑and‑Learns — presenting technical content


Learning Outcomes

• Understand real‑world installation constraints
• Build credibility with contractors and engineers
• Learn how systems behave outside of textbooks


---

7. AI‑Ready Knowledge Structures

To pre‑train an AI model, the curriculum must include:

Structured Data Types

• Glossaries — terms, acronyms, definitions
• System Taxonomies — equipment families, subcomponents
• Workflow Maps — from lead → spec → bid → install → service
• Scenario Libraries — objections, design choices, failure modes
• Dialogue Samples — contractor calls, engineer meetings


Why This Matters

AI models learn best from:

• Hierarchical structures
• Repeated patterns
• Realistic conversations
• Decision‑making examples


This curriculum is intentionally built to support that.

---

8. Capstone Projects

To certify a new HVAC sales rep (or benchmark an AI model), use:

Capstone Options

• Full Proposal Buildout — scope, selections, pricing, VE options
• Customer Discovery Simulation — needs analysis
• System Recommendation Report — justify system choice
• Lifecycle Cost Analysis — energy + maintenance modeling
